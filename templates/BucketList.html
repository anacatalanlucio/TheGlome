The Bucket List

I am currently writing a bucket list for the next years of my life. I am currently 24 years old. I had planned that by my 25th birthday I would have graduated from college and I would start to undertake bigger projects. For me a bucket list is a way to keep track of the accomplishments that you want to meet during your lifetime. Some items in the list are personal and others are more professional. Some items seem unattainable but exciting. Some items are more simple than others. It is important to keep in mind that the bucket list is for you. You are allowed to change it and modify it as you wish. It is important to accept that failure will be part of it. In case failure comes, imagine yourself being very old but satisfied with the idea that at least you tried. Lots of people do not even try. Trying is an accomplishment. Therefore, failing is an accomplishment.


While doing my bucket list I thought that I should also do a bucket list of the things I have accomplished in the past. I think it is very important to retrospect when planning future items for two reasons. One is that you can be grateful for what you already have accomplished. It does not have to be huge projects; small victories count too. You can be proud of the things that took courage to accomplish. Another reason is that it gives you a sense of accomplishment and gives you confidence to achieve more. Looking at past small victories tells you that you are very capable to undertake future plans. If you cannot think of something you accomplished in the past. Start keeping track of your accomplishments today. Your first item on your list can be: Do a bucket list for my future life. Starting Tip: If the items in your bucket list seem impossible to achieve, Tomorrow plan a ToDo list. Add one item to your list. Do the thing and check the item. After tomorrow, plan another ToDo List. Add two items to your list. Do them and check each item. And so on... Prove yourself that you can commit to yourself. Prove yourself that you are capable to undertake small steps. The bigger steps will come with constance. Wake up thinking that at night you will go to bed with satisfaction knowing that your items in the list have been checked.
